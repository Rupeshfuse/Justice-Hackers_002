Introduction

This project showcases the evolution of UI/UX design over the decades, highlighting the significant trends and technologies that have shaped the web as we know it today.

Project Purpose

This exhibit aims to educate and inspire visitors about the principles, technologies, and trends that have shaped UI/UX design over the decades. Whether you're a student, a design enthusiast, or a professional in the field, this exhibit offers insights into how design aesthetics and user experiences have evolved alongside technological advancements.

Overview of Eras Covered

  • 1990s: The Birth of the Web

    • Witness the early days of the World Wide Web with basic HTML layouts and inline styles.
    • Discover how websites were structured using tables and minimal design aesthetics.

  • 2000s: Rise of CSS and Flash

    • Experience the transition to CSS for layout and styling, replacing table-based designs.
    • Explore the impact of Flash technology on interactive content and multimedia experiences.

  • 2010s: Era of Responsive Design

    • Learn about the emergence of responsive design principles to adapt websites for various devices.
    • See the shift towards flat design aesthetics, focusing on simplicity and usability.

  • 2020s: Modern Trends and Innovations

    • Delve into material design principles, neomorphism, and glassmorphism for immersive user interfaces.
    • Experience dynamic animations, scroll effects, and the introduction of light/dark modes.

  • 2030s: Future Speculations

    • Imagine speculative design concepts such as paper morphism and high contrast interfaces.
    • Explore how emerging technologies like AR/VR are shaping the future of UI/UX design.

Navigation

Use the timeline navigation menu above to jump directly to any era of interest. Each era-specific page offers visual examples, detailed explanations, and insights into the design trends and technologies prevalent during that time.

Get Inspired

Whether you're here to learn, research, or simply marvel at the evolution of design, we hope this exhibit sparks your curiosity and appreciation for the art and science of UI/UX design.
